# Playbook: Vendor Work Order
## CrownKey Email Guardian — Template v1.0

**Situation:** Crown Key is dispatching a vendor to perform work at a managed property. This is the work order communication sent to the vendor. It must contain all the information the vendor needs to perform the work without a follow-up call. Two versions are provided: Standard (non-urgent) and Urgent/Emergency.

**Recipient Type:** Vendor

**Risk Level:** Low for routine; Medium for emergencies (wrong or incomplete information causes delays in habitability repairs, which carries legal risk).

**What Mason Needs to Check:**
- Confirm that authorization language accurately reflects Crown Key's authority and the management agreement's maintenance threshold.
- Confirm that work order language does not inadvertently create a contractual commitment beyond what is intended (e.g., guarantee of a specific scope of work before a technician has assessed the situation).
- For emergency work orders: confirm that the authorization language is sufficient to allow the vendor to proceed without a separate written agreement, consistent with Crown Key's vendor contracts.

## Template A — Standard Work Order

**Subject:** Work Order — [Property Address] — [Brief Issue Description]

---

**CROWN KEY REALTY — WORK ORDER**

**Date Issued:** [Date]
**Work Order Reference:** WO-[Number or Date Code]
**Priority:** Standard

---

**Property**
Address: [Full Property Address]
Unit: [Unit Number, or "N/A" for single-family]
Tenant Name: [Tenant Name]
Tenant Contact: [Tenant Phone] / [Tenant Email]

**Access Instructions**
[Describe access method: lockbox code, tenant will provide access, key at office, etc. Be specific.]
Example: "Tenant will provide access. Please call [Tenant Name] at [Tenant Phone] at least [X] hours in advance to schedule."
Or: "Lockbox on front door, code is [Code]. Please notify tenant before entering via text to [Tenant Phone]."

---

**Work Required**
[Detailed description of what needs to be done. Be specific enough that the vendor arrives knowing what to expect.]

Example:
- Inspect and repair leaking kitchen faucet (tenant reports drip from the base of the faucet handle — hot water side)
- Check under-sink supply lines for corrosion while on site

---

**Timeline**
Please complete this work within [X] business days of receiving this work order.
If you are unable to meet this timeline, please contact us immediately at [Manager Phone].

---

**Authorization**
You are authorized to proceed with the work described above. For materials and labor within [dollar threshold], no additional authorization is required. For costs exceeding [dollar threshold], please contact [Manager Name] before proceeding.

---

**On Completion**
1. Notify [Manager Name] by [phone/email] that work is complete.
2. Submit your invoice to [Invoice Contact Email] referencing WO-[Number].
3. Invoice payment terms: [Net 15 / Net 30 / as per vendor agreement].

---

Questions? Contact:
[Manager Name]
[Phone]
[Email]

## Work Description Writing Guide

The work description is the most important field. Use these principles:

**Specific over general.** "Leaking kitchen faucet, hot water side, drip from base of handle" not "fix faucet."

**Location within the unit.** Always specify which room, which appliance, which area. Vendors work multiple properties — they cannot guess.

**What the tenant reported vs. what Crown Key observed.** If the description is based on a tenant report, say so: "Tenant reports..." If Crown Key observed it directly, say so: "Observed by maintenance coordinator during inspection on [date]."

**Scope of what is authorized.** If Crown Key wants the vendor to check related systems while on site (e.g., check all under-sink supply lines while fixing the faucet), say so explicitly. This prevents a second trip and helps the vendor do complete work.

---

## Usage Notes

- Every work order should have a reference number. Create a simple numbering system (date-based or sequential) and use it consistently.
- Send work orders to one vendor at a time. Do not copy competing vendors on the same email.
- For tenant-access work, the vendor must give advance notice before entering. Crown Key's standard is [X hours/days] — confirm this requirement and include it in the work order.
- For emergency work orders after hours: ensure the vendor has a direct cell number for the on-call Crown Key contact, not just an office line.
- File a copy of every work order in Property Meld and in the property's records.
