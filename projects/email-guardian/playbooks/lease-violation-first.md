# Playbook: Lease Violation — First Notice
## CrownKey Email Guardian — Template v1.0

**Situation:** A tenant has committed a lease violation — examples include unauthorized pets, unauthorized occupants, noise complaints, parking violations, smoking where prohibited, or damage to the property. This is the first written notice of the violation. The tone is professional and factual, not accusatory. The tenant is given a cure period (a window of time to fix the problem).

**Recipient Type:** Tenant

**Risk Level:** Medium-High — lease violation notices are legal documents and must be handled precisely. The wrong language could invalidate the notice or create Fair Housing exposure.

**Crown Key Policy (Attorney Reviewed):**
- This email is a supplemental communication — it is NOT the formal legal notice. Formal lease violation notices are served separately via first-class mail and/or personal delivery (post and mail). Email alone does not constitute valid legal service under Crown Key's leases.
- All Crown Key properties are exempt from AB 1482 (California Tenant Protection Act).
- Attorney review complete. This template is approved for use.

**IMPORTANT — Assistance Animals:** If the violation involves an unauthorized pet that may be an assistance/support animal, DO NOT send this template. Route to Tier 3 (Susan) immediately. This situation requires individual legal review before any written communication.

**Usage Note:** Send this email at the same time formal written notice is served. Use the correct cure period per the violation type — see Crown Key's notice reference guide.

If we do not receive confirmation that this matter has been resolved by [Cure Deadline Date], Crown Key Realty will be required to take further action as permitted under your lease agreement and California law.

## Violation Description Writing Guide

The description of the violation is the highest-risk field in this template. Use these rules:

**Write what was observed, not what you concluded.**
- Correct: "An unauthorized pet was observed in the unit during a maintenance visit on [date]."
- Incorrect: "You are harboring an unauthorized animal."

**Reference the source neutrally.**
- Correct: "Crown Key received a complaint regarding excessive noise levels on [date]."
- Incorrect: "Your neighbor complained about you."

**Do not add characterizations.**
- Correct: "Smoking was observed in the unit."
- Incorrect: "You have been repeatedly violating the no-smoking policy."

**Never reference a protected class.**
- Never mention the source of a complaint in a way that identifies a neighbor's protected class.
- Never describe a violation in terms that imply the tenant's behavior is related to their culture, religion, national origin, or any other protected characteristic.

## Usage Notes

- This template covers first-notice situations only. If a second notice is required (violation not cured or recurring), escalate to Susan and consult with Mason before sending a second notice.
- If the violation involves potential criminal activity, do not use this template. Contact Susan immediately.

- Document the date and method of sending. Keep a copy in the tenant's file.
