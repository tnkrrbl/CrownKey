# Playbook: Lease Renewal Offer
## CrownKey Email Guardian — Template v1.0

**Situation:** A tenant's lease is approaching its expiration date (typically 60 days out) and Crown Key is offering them the opportunity to renew. The tone is warm and appreciative — renewing a good tenant is good business and should feel that way. The email clearly states the new terms and makes it easy for the tenant to respond.

**Recipient Type:** Tenant

**Risk Level:** Medium — renewal offers must include accurate terms and must not make promises or representations that differ from the actual lease document. The timing of this notice may also have legal requirements.

**Crown Key Policy (Attorney Reviewed):**
- All Crown Key properties are exempt from AB 1482 (California Tenant Protection Act). Rent increase caps under AB 1482 do not apply to this portfolio.
- Livermore properties: Excluded from local rent control considerations per Crown Key portfolio policy.
- Tracy, Manteca, Lathrop, Mountain House, Ripon, River Islands, Tracy Hills: No local rent control ordinances applicable to Crown Key's properties.
- Attorney review complete. This template is approved for use.

**Usage Notes:**
- Send 60 days before lease expiration (standard Crown Key policy).
- Rent increase of 10% or more requires 90 days notice — verify the increase amount before sending.
- This email offers renewal — it does not guarantee it. Renewal is subject to a signed agreement.

## Tone Notes

- The opening should feel warm — "we'd love to have you stay" is appropriate when the tenancy has been in good standing.
- The renewal terms table should be easy to read at a glance. The tenant should immediately see what changes (rent) and what stays the same (everything else unless noted).
- The call to action is binary: accept or let us know you're not renewing. Make it easy to do either.
- Do not include anything in this email that could be read as pressuring the tenant to renew or suggesting any negative consequence for choosing not to renew beyond the normal move-out process.

## Rent Increase Compliance Note (for Q / Builder)

When this template is populated by the Email Guardian AI, the system must check:

1. Is the property in a jurisdiction with local rent control? (Confirm list with Mason.)
2. Is the property covered by California's AB 1482 (statewide rent cap)? (Single-family homes with a tenancy of 12+ months, multi-family built before 2005, generally covered — confirm with Mason per property type.)
3. Does the proposed rent increase comply with the applicable cap?

If a proposed rent exceeds the cap, the Email Guardian must flag the email and prevent sending until reviewed.

## Usage Notes

- This template should be sent approximately 60 days before the lease expiration.
- A second reminder may be sent 30 days before if no response is received. Use a modified version of this template with a more urgent tone.
- If the tenant does not respond by the deadline, escalate to Susan for follow-up. Do not automatically convert to month-to-month without owner awareness.
- If the renewal includes any changes to the lease terms beyond rent (pet policy, parking, etc.), include those changes explicitly in the email and in the new lease agreement.
