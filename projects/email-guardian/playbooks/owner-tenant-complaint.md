# Playbook: Owner Notification — Tenant Complaint or Issue
## CrownKey Email Guardian — Template v1.0

**Situation:** A tenant has raised a complaint, reported a dispute with a neighbor, expressed dissatisfaction, or flagged a concern that does not require immediate repair authorization but that the owner should be aware of. Crown Key is informing the owner, sharing the facts, and explaining what Crown Key is doing about it.

**Recipient Type:** Property Owner

**Risk Level:** Medium — the way this email is written affects how the owner perceives Crown Key's competence. It also creates a record that may be relevant if the complaint escalates into a legal matter.

**What Mason Needs to Check:**
- Confirm that sharing tenant complaint details with the owner does not create tenant privacy issues. In general, owners have the right to know about issues at their property — but the level of detail and the tenant's identifying information should be calibrated appropriately.
- Confirm that the email does not characterize the tenant's complaint in a way that could be read as disparaging or retaliatory.
- If the complaint relates to a potential Fair Housing issue (e.g., harassment, discrimination complaint against a neighbor), this template is NOT appropriate — consult Mason before responding.
- Confirm that Crown Key's described response is accurate and consistent with legal obligations (e.g., if a repair is involved, confirm the legal timeline).

## Complaint Description Writing Guide

The complaint description is sensitive territory. Use these rules:

**Neutral and factual.** Do not characterize the tenant as difficult, demanding, or unreasonable — even if Crown Key believes that privately. The record should reflect what was reported, not Crown Key's opinion of the reporter.

**No protected class references.** Do not mention the complaining tenant's or neighboring tenant's race, national origin, religion, disability, familial status, or any other protected class characteristic. This is especially critical in neighbor disputes.

**What was reported, not what you concluded.** "The tenant reported excessive noise" — not "the neighbor is definitely playing music too loud."

**Do not identify the complaining tenant to the neighbor** (if the complaint involves another tenant) in ways that could create a retaliation risk. If the complaint involves a potential harassment situation, stop and consult Mason before writing anything.

---

## Tone Notes

- The structure of this email is: situation → Crown Key's response → context → owner's ask (if any).
- Crown Key's response should be described in past or present tense ("we have contacted," "we are scheduling") — not future tense ("we will look into it"). Proactive management means Crown Key is already in motion.
- If Crown Key does not yet have a full plan, say so honestly, but explain what the next step is and when the owner will hear back. Do not go silent on an unresolved situation.

---

## Usage Notes

- If the tenant complaint involves a Fair Housing concern (harassment, discrimination by a neighbor or Crown Key employee), do not use this template. Consult Mason immediately.
- If the complaint is about a habitability condition, route to the repair templates instead.
- Keep a copy of this email in the property file. If the situation escalates, this becomes part of the documentation chain.
- If this is the second or third complaint of the same nature, the owner-bad-tenant-pattern template may be more appropriate.
