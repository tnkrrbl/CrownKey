# Playbook: Owner Notification — Pattern of Tenant Issues
## CrownKey Email Guardian — Template v1.0

**Situation:** A tenant has a recurring or escalating pattern of issues — multiple late payments, repeated lease violations, ongoing neighbor complaints, non-compliance with notices, property damage, or a combination of these. This email informs the owner of the pattern, explains Crown Key's current position and documentation, and presents options (up to and potentially including non-renewal or legal action). This is a serious communication that requires Crown Key to sound like the expert, not like someone venting about a tenant.

**Recipient Type:** Property Owner

**Risk Level:** High — this email documents a pattern that may lead to legal action (non-renewal, eviction). The record created here matters. Any characterization of the tenant must be strictly factual, tied to documented incidents, and free of any protected class language.

**Crown Key Policy (Attorney Reviewed):**
- All Crown Key properties are exempt from AB 1482 (California Tenant Protection Act). Just Cause eviction restrictions do not apply to this portfolio.
- Attorney review complete. This template is approved for use.

**Required before sending:**
- Every incident cited must be documented in Crown Key's files (notices sent, dates, tenant responses).
- If the tenant recently filed a habitability complaint or submitted an accommodation request, route to Tier 3 (Susan) before sending — retaliation timing creates legal risk regardless of AB 1482 exemption.
- Tenant description must be strictly factual — tied to documented incidents only. No characterizations that reference or imply protected class characteristics.

## Issue Description Writing Guide

Every item in the pattern summary must meet this standard:

**Documented.** If it is not in Crown Key's file with a date, a notice, or a written record — it does not go in the pattern summary. "We think the tenant has had parties" is not appropriate. "A noise complaint was received on [date] and a reminder notice was sent on [date]" is appropriate.

**Factual, not characterizing.** Do not use adjectives like "difficult," "uncooperative," "disrespectful," or "problem tenant." Describe the event and the response. Let the pattern speak for itself.

**Zero protected class language.** This is the highest-risk email for discriminatory framing. Never reference the tenant's national origin, religion, race, disability, family status, or any other protected characteristic — even indirectly. If the issues involve a person's behavior related to a disability accommodation, stop and consult Mason before proceeding.

---

## Usage Notes

- This email should only be sent after Crown Key has a documented pattern — not after a single incident.
- Do not use this template to communicate issues to the wrong owner (e.g., if the complaint involves a multi-unit property and the issue is with another owner's tenant).
- Retain this email and the full documentation file. If legal action follows, this becomes part of the evidence package.
- If the tenant has recently filed a habitability complaint, a Fair Housing complaint, or any other complaint against Crown Key or the owner, consult Mason before sending anything that could be read as retaliatory.
