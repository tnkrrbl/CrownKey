# Playbook: Owner Notification — Repair Under $500
## CrownKey Email Guardian — Template v1.0

**Situation:** A repair has been completed (or is in progress) at an owner's property and the cost falls within the pre-authorized maintenance threshold (typically under $500 or as specified in the management agreement). The owner does not need to approve this — Crown Key is authorized to act. This email informs the owner proactively and positions Crown Key as a competent manager of their investment.

**Recipient Type:** Property Owner

**Risk Level:** Low — this is an informational update. No approval required.

**What Mason Needs to Check:**
- Confirm the management agreement's authorized repair threshold. The template references "your maintenance authorization threshold" — this must match the actual agreement.
- Confirm that the work described is within the scope of what Crown Key is authorized to handle without owner approval.
- Confirm there is no language that could be read as limiting Crown Key's liability in ways inconsistent with the management agreement.

---

## Email Template

**Subject:** Maintenance Update — [Property Address]

---

Hi [Owner First Name],

Quick update on your property at [Property Address].

Our team identified [brief description of the issue, e.g., "a failing garbage disposal in the kitchen"] and took care of it this week. The repair has been completed by [Vendor Name] and the unit is back in full working order.

**Work completed:** [Description of repair]
**Completed by:** [Vendor Name]
**Date completed:** [Date]
**Cost:** $[Amount]

This falls within your pre-authorized maintenance threshold, so no action is needed on your end. The charge will appear on your [Month] owner statement.

This type of routine maintenance keeps your property in excellent condition and prevents small issues from becoming more expensive repairs down the road.

Let me know if you have any questions.

[Manager First Name]
Crown Key Realty
[Phone Number]
[Email Address]

## Tone Notes

- This email should feel like a brief, confident update — not an apology or a problem report.
- Lead with what was done, not with the problem. "We took care of X" not "There was a problem with X."
- Tie the maintenance to investment protection in the final paragraph. This reinforces Crown Key's value.
- Keep it short. Owners do not need a detailed narrative for a routine repair. Get in, give the key facts, get out.

---

## Usage Notes

- Only use this template when the cost is within the management agreement's authorized threshold. If over threshold, use the over-$500 template.
- Send this email after the repair is completed, not before — unless the timeline requires advance notice to the owner for scheduling access.
- Attach the vendor invoice if Crown Key's practice is to share invoices with owners at this cost level. Confirm with Susan.
- If the repair reveals a larger underlying issue (e.g., the disposal replacement revealed plumbing corrosion), flag that separately in the same email or a follow-up before the owner sees the owner statement.
