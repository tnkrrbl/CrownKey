# Playbook: Move-Out Charge Dispute Response
## CrownKey Email Guardian — Template v1.0

**Situation:** A former tenant has contested some or all of the charges deducted from their security deposit (or billed to them after move-out). This is the response email. The tone acknowledges their concern, explains the factual and legal basis for each charge, and explains next steps clearly.

**Recipient Type:** Former Tenant (Tenant Voice standards apply)

**Risk Level:** High — security deposit disputes are a common source of small claims court actions in California. Anything written here could be used as evidence. Every claim must be accurate, documented, and legally defensible.

**What Mason Needs to Check:**
- Confirm the itemized charge language complies with California Civil Code Section 1950.5 (the security deposit statute), specifically that each deduction is permitted under the statute.
- Confirm the distinction between "normal wear and tear" (not chargeable) and damage (chargeable) is correctly applied to each charge listed.
- Confirm the timeline stated for sending the initial itemization complied with California's 21-day requirement.
- Confirm that the response language does not inadvertently waive any of Crown Key's claims or admit liability.
- Confirm the dispute resolution process described (Small Claims Court reference, etc.) is accurate and current.
- Flag if any charges relate to cleaning, painting, or carpet — these are frequently contested and have specific California case law.

## Charge Description Writing Guide

Every charge description must be:

**Factual:** "Carpet in bedroom 1 had multiple stains not present at move-in (see photo A-7 from move-out inspection)" — not "carpet was filthy."

**Referenced:** Tie every charge to documentation. "Per invoice from [Vendor] dated [Date], cleaning cost was $[X]."

**Distinguished from normal wear and tear:** Normal wear and tear includes minor scuffs on walls, small nail holes from hanging pictures, carpet wear from normal foot traffic, fading from sunlight. These cannot be charged. Damage includes stains, burns, holes, broken fixtures, unauthorized alterations.

**Consistent with move-in documentation:** If the move-in report shows a condition, that condition cannot be charged at move-out.

---

## Usage Notes

- Do not send this email without first reviewing the complete move-in and move-out documentation.
- Do not include any charges that are not supported by documentation.
- Do not include language that admits fault or error unless Crown Key is actually agreeing to revise a charge.
- If Crown Key determines that a charge was made in error, issue a corrected itemization and revised check rather than burying it in dispute correspondence.
- Retain this email and all documentation in the tenant's closed file for a minimum period per California records retention requirements. Confirm retention period with Mason.
