# Playbook: Owner Notification — Repair Over $500 (Approval Required)
## CrownKey Email Guardian — Template v1.0

**Situation:** A repair has been identified at an owner's property and the estimated cost exceeds the pre-authorized maintenance threshold (typically $500 or as specified in the management agreement). Crown Key cannot proceed without the owner's approval. This email informs the owner of the situation, provides context and options, makes a recommendation, and asks for a clear authorization to proceed.

**Recipient Type:** Property Owner

**Risk Level:** Medium — this email requires a clear authorization record. The approval must be explicit and documented. There may also be habitability or legal urgency depending on the nature of the repair.

**What Mason Needs to Check:**
- Confirm that the template's reference to California habitability law is accurate and that timelines stated are consistent with actual legal requirements.
- Confirm that waiting for owner approval is legally permissible given the nature of the repair. Some repairs (habitability issues) may require Crown Key to proceed regardless of owner approval and then notify the owner.
- Confirm that the email creates a clear, documented record of the approval request that would hold up if the owner later disputes Crown Key's authority to proceed.
- Confirm that Crown Key's recommendation is presented as a recommendation, not a commitment, until the owner approves.

## Email Template

**Subject:** Authorization Needed — Repair at [Property Address] (Est. $[Amount])

---

Hi [Owner First Name],

I wanted to reach out immediately regarding a maintenance situation at [Property Address] that requires your authorization to proceed.

**The Issue**
[Clear description of the problem in plain terms. What is broken, what caused it if known, and what happens if it is not addressed. Example: "The HVAC system has failed and is not producing heat or cooling. Our technician confirmed the compressor has reached end of life and replacement is necessary."]

[If there is a habitability or legal urgency, state it here: "Because this affects the habitability of the unit, California law requires us to address it promptly. We want to move as quickly as possible once we have your authorization."]

**Your Options**

| Option | Description | Estimated Cost | Our Assessment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Option A | [Description, e.g., "Repair the existing unit"] | $[Amount] | [Pros and cons] |

**Our Recommendation**

We recommend [Option A or B]. [One to three sentences explaining the reasoning — tie it to cost over time, warranty, protecting the property's value, or avoiding repeat repairs. ## Tone Notes

- Lead with the situation, not with an apology. Owners expect problems to arise — they want to know Crown Key is on top of it.
- The recommendation must be specific and confident. "We recommend Option B" — not "either option could work" or "it's really up to you." Crown Key is the expert.
- Make the authorization action frictionless. "Reply with 'Approved — Option B'" is easier than asking the owner to call or fill out a form.
- If there is genuine urgency (habitability, risk of further damage, tenant discomfort), state it factually — once. Do not dramatize.

---

## Usage Notes

- If the repair is a habitability issue under California law (heating, water, weatherproofing, etc.), consult Mason before delaying work solely to wait for owner approval. Crown Key may have a legal obligation to proceed and then bill the owner.
- Save the owner's email reply authorizing the work in the property's file. This is the authorization record.
- If the owner is unreachable for a habitability emergency, document the attempts to reach them before proceeding. Consult Mason on the correct protocol for each emergency type.
- If the owner refuses to authorize a habitability repair, consult Mason immediately.
