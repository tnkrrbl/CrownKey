# The Wolf Pack — Your First Session With Claude
### What to Actually Do After Setup

---

You've got Claude running. Now what?

This guide gives you 5 starter prompts that will get you real results
in your first 30 minutes. Pick the one that solves the most annoying
problem in your day right now.

> **Before you use these prompts:** Make sure you've filled in your tech stack
> in `CLAUDE.md`. Claude reads that file at the start of every session — if you've
> told it you use AppFolio and Gmail, it will automatically suggest how to connect
> those tools instead of building something generic. The prompts below work without
> it, but they work *much better* with it filled in.

---

## Starter Prompt 1 — Lease Expiration Tracker

Copy and paste this to Claude:

> I have tenants with leases that expire at different times and I'm always
> scrambling at the last minute. I want a simple way to see which leases
> expire in the next 90 days. I track everything in a spreadsheet right now.
>
> Can you help me build a simple tool where I can enter my tenant names,
> unit numbers, and lease end dates — and it shows me a sorted list of
> who's expiring soonest? Start simple. I just want to see the list.

---

## Starter Prompt 2 — Maintenance Request Log

> I get maintenance requests from tenants by text and email and I lose track
> of them. I want a simple log where I can paste in a request and it saves:
> - The tenant name and unit
> - What the problem is
> - The date I received it
> - Whether it's been fixed yet
>
> I want to be able to look at a list of all open requests. Keep it simple —
> I don't need anything fancy, just something I can actually use tomorrow.

---

## Starter Prompt 3 — Late Rent Notice Template

> I need to send late rent notices to tenants when they're past due.
> Right now I write them from scratch each time, which is annoying.
>
> Can you write me a professional but firm late rent notice template?
> It should have blank spaces for the tenant name, unit, amount owed,
> and due date. Make it sound like it came from a real property management
> company, not a robot. I'll copy and paste it into email.

---

## Starter Prompt 4 — Owner Monthly Report

> Every month I have to send my property owners a summary of what happened
> with their property. It takes me way too long.
>
> Can you help me create a template for a monthly owner report that covers:
> - Rent collected this month
> - Any maintenance issues and their status
> - Current vacancy status
> - Anything the owner needs to know or approve
>
> I want it to look professional. I'll fill in the numbers and you format it
> into a clean report I can email.

---

## Starter Prompt 5 — Tenant Communication Templates

> I send the same types of emails over and over to tenants:
> lease renewal reminders, maintenance confirmations, move-out instructions,
> late payment warnings, etc.
>
> Can you write me a set of 5 professional email templates for these
> common situations? I want them to be warm but professional — I want
> tenants to feel like they're working with a real person, not a form letter.

## Tips for Working With Claude

**Be specific about what you want.** The more detail you give, the better
Claude can help. "Help me with leases" is vague. "Show me leases expiring
in the next 60 days, sorted by date" is clear.

**Tell Claude what you don't want.** "Keep it simple," "don't use code I
have to run," "make it something I can copy into Excel" — all of these help.

**If you don't like the answer, say so.** Just say "that's too complicated"
or "make it shorter" or "can you do this a different way?" Claude won't
take it personally.

**Save what works.** When Claude gives you a template or a process you like,
paste it into a Google Doc. Build your library over time.
